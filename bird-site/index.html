Bird Migration & Highway Noise

Exploring how traffic sounds shape avian paths

"The early bird catches the worm — but not when the highway drowns it out."

Problem & Motivation

Bird migration routes are influenced by a variety of environmental factors. One under-explored factor is highway noise, which can disrupt nesting, feeding, and communication behaviors. Our project seeks to uncover how traffic corridors impact bird migration patterns across seasons.

Our Solution

We combined bird observation data with highway noise projections to identify key regions of disruption. Our interactive map and data pipeline help visualize and interpret these impacts for both researchers and the public.

Data Sources

  • eBird API – bird observation data from Cornell Lab
  • FHWA TNM Models – highway noise projections
  • OpenStreetMap – basemap & location data

Modeling Bird Populations with XGBoost

To estimate how changing noise conditions might impact future bird populations, we trained an XGBoost regression model using:

  • Location-based bird observation counts (from eBird)
  • Ambient and projected noise levels (in dB)
  • Environmental features like extreme weather

The model was able to predict population changes with a mean absolute error under xx% on held-out validation data. By simulating increased or reduced highway noise scenarios, we projected localized bird population shifts—highlighting areas most sensitive to acoustic disruption.

These predictions are layered into our interactive map to visually represent both current and projected migration patterns.
